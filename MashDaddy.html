Not just because it served our ancestors for thousands of  years, but because it continues to do what no other cooking preparation technique does: mashing plant fibers and crushing the juice from plant cells to fundamentally transform their texture and release their full aroma, nutrients and flavor. 30 Low Carb foods made with a mortar and pestle:

Pesto - a sauce made from basil, garlic, pine nuts, parmesan cheese, and olive oil.
Guacamole - a dip made from mashed avocado, onion, cilantro, tomato, and lime juice.
Salsa - a sauce made from chopped tomatoes, onion, chili peppers, and cilantro.
Curry paste - a blend of spices, herbs, and aromatics used as a base for curry dishes.
Hummus - a dip made from mashed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic.
Chimichurri - a sauce made from parsley, garlic, vinegar, and olive oil, commonly used on grilled meats.
Harissa - a North African spice paste made from roasted chili peppers, garlic, and spices.
Garam masala - a spice blend used in Indian cuisine, made from whole spices that are toasted and ground.
Tapenade - a spread made from olives, capers, anchovies, and olive oil.
Adobo - a seasoning blend used in Latin American and Filipino cuisine, made from garlic, vinegar, and spices.
Aioli - a sauce made from garlic, egg yolks, lemon juice, and olive oil, commonly used on sandwiches and as a dip.
Romesco sauce - a sauce made from roasted red peppers, almonds, bread crumbs, and garlic, commonly used in Spanish cuisine.
Breadcrumbs - made by crushing stale bread in a mortar and pestle, used as a coating for fried foods or as a topping for casseroles.
Dukkah - an Egyptian spice blend made from toasted nuts, seeds, and spices, commonly used as a dip with bread and olive oil.
Curry powder - a blend of spices used in Indian and Southeast Asian cuisine, made from toasted and ground spices like coriander, cumin, and turmeric.
Piri piri sauce - a spicy sauce made from African bird's eye chili peppers, garlic, and vinegar, commonly used in Portuguese and African cuisine.
Za'atar - a Middle Eastern spice blend made from thyme, sumac, sesame seeds, and salt, commonly used as a seasoning for bread or vegetables.
Sofrito - a base sauce used in Latin American and Spanish cuisine, tomatoes, onions, peppers, garlic, and herbs, used to flavor rice, beans, and stews.
Ras el hanout - a North African spice blend made from a variety of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger, commonly used in tagines and stews.
Rasam powder - a spice blend used in South Indian cuisine, toasted and ground spices cumin, coriander, peppercorns, used to flavor soups and stews.
Gochujang - a Korean chili paste made from fermented soybeans, chili peppers, and rice, used as a marinade or sauce for meats and vegetables.
Curry laksa paste - a Malaysian spice paste used to make laksa soup, made from lemongrass, galangal, chili peppers, and shrimp paste.
Chili oil - a Chinese condiment made from dried chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, and oil, commonly used as a topping for noodles or dumplings.
Massaman curry paste - a Thai curry paste made from roasted spices like cumin and coriander, and aromatics like lemongrass and galangal.
Anchovy paste - a condiment made from mashed anchovies, used to add umami flavor to dishes like Caesar salad dressing or pasta sauces.
Black garlic paste - made by fermenting garlic until it turns black and becomes sweet and earthy, used as a seasoning for meats, vegetables, or sauces.
Pistou - a French sauce similar to pesto but without the nuts, made from basil, garlic, and olive oil, used as a topping for soups or on bread.
Sambal - a spicy Indonesian condiment made from chili peppers, shrimp paste, and vinegar, used as a dip or seasoning for meats or vegetables.
Mole sauce - a Mexican sauce made from roasted chili peppers, nuts, seeds, and chocolate, used as a topping for meats or as a dip for tortilla chips.
Togarashi - a Japanese spice blend made from chili peppers, sesame seeds, and seaweed, commonly used as a seasoning for rice dishes or grilled meats.

These are just a few examples of the wide variety of foods that can be made using a mortar and pestle, as the grinding and pounding motion can be used to break down and blend ingredients in unique and flavorful ways.
