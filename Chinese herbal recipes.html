Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) uses a mortar and pestle to grind, crush, and blend herbs for teas, pastes, pills, and decoctions. Here are 50 of the most popular herbal recipes and uses:

1. Herbal Teas & Decoctions (汤剂, Tang Ji)

These are brewed by boiling herbs in water to extract their medicinal properties.

  1. Ba Zhen Tang (Eight Treasures Decoction, 八珍汤) – Tonifies blood and Qi with ginseng, angelica root, and poria.

  2. Liu Wei Di Huang Tang (Six-Ingredient Rehmannia Decoction, 六味地黄汤) – Kidney Yin tonic with rehmannia, cornelian cherry, and Chinese yam.

  3. Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan (Golden Cabinet Kidney Qi Pills, 金匮肾气丸) – Kidney Yang tonic with cinnamon bark and aconite.

  4. Xiao Yao San (Free and Easy Wanderer, 逍遥散) – Regulates liver Qi with bupleurum, peony root, and licorice.

  5. Gui Pi Tang (Restore the Spleen Decoction, 归脾汤) – Boosts blood and Qi, often used for anemia or insomnia.

  6. Si Wu Tang (Four Substances Decoction, 四物汤) – Blood-nourishing formula with angelica, peony, ligusticum, and rehmannia.

  7. Long Dan Xie Gan Tang (Gentiana Liver Drain Decoction, 龙胆泻肝汤) – Clears liver fire, used for migraines and red eyes.

  8. Ban Xia Hou Po Tang (Pinellia and Magnolia Bark Decoction, 半夏厚朴汤) – Treats throat obstruction ("plum pit Qi").

  9. Zuo Gui Wan (Left Restoring Kidney Pill, 左归丸) – Nourishes kidney Yin, often for night sweats.

  10. You Gui Wan (Right Restoring Kidney Pill, 右归丸) – Warms kidney Yang with cinnamon and aconite.

2. Herbal Pastes & Ointments (膏方, Gao Fang)

Ground herbs mixed with honey, sesame oil, or vinegar to make pastes.

  1. Yunnan Baiyao (云南白药) – Famous wound-healing powder for injuries.

  2. Huang Lian Gao (黄连膏) – Antibacterial ointment with coptis root.

  3. Zi Cao Gao (紫草膏) – Purple gromwell salve for burns and eczema.

  4. Ma Ying Long Hemorrhoid Ointment (马应龙痔疮膏) – Soothing ointment for hemorrhoids.

  5. Fu Zi Li Zhong Gao (附子理中膏) – Warming paste for digestive issues.

  6. Sheng Ma Gao (升麻膏) – Paste for skin rashes and sunburns.

  7. Er Cha Gao (儿茶膏) – Astringent paste for sores and ulcers.

  8. San Huang Gao (三黄膏) – Anti-inflammatory salve with rhubarb, coptis, and phellodendron.

  9. Jin Huang Gao (金黄膏) – Turmeric-based paste for swelling and infections.

  10. Dang Gui Gao (当归膏) – Angelica root paste for blood circulation.

3. Herbal Powders (散剂, San Ji)

Ground herbs for direct consumption, capsules, or mixing into water.

  1. Bai Zi Yang Xin San (柏子养心散) – Calms the mind, often used for anxiety.

  2. Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi San (香砂六君子散) – Digestive aid for bloating.

  3. Wu Wei Zi San (五味子散) – Schisandra berry powder for lung health.

  4. Shi Quan Da Bu San (十全大补散) – General tonic for energy and strength.

  5. Chuan Bei Mu Powder (川贝母粉) – Fritillaria bulb for chronic coughs.

  6. Ping Wei San (平胃散) – Drains dampness for stomach issues.

  7. Zhi Bai Di Huang San (知柏地黄散) – Clears heat, used for menopause symptoms.

  8. Ge Gen San (葛根散) – Kudzu root powder for colds and neck tension.

  9. Bu Zhong Yi Qi San (补中益气散) – Raises Qi, good for fatigue.

  10. Shen Ling Bai Zhu San (参苓白术散) – Strengthens digestion and stops diarrhea.

4. Pills & Boluses (丸剂, Wan Ji)

Ground herbs mixed with honey or water to form small pills.

  1. An Shen Bu Xin Wan (安神补心丸) – Calms the heart and mind.

  2. Tian Wang Bu Xin Dan (天王补心丹) – Nourishes heart blood for insomnia.

  3. Xiao Feng Wan (消风丸) – Treats skin itching and eczema.

  4. Chai Hu Shu Gan Wan (柴胡疏肝丸) – Soothes liver Qi stagnation.

  5. Shou Wu Wan (首乌丸) – Polygonum multiflorum formula for hair growth.

  6. Zhen Zhu An Chuang Wan (珍珠暗疮丸) – Pearl powder for acne.

  7. Shen Nong Bai Ren Wan (神农百仁丸) – Longevity tonic.

  8. Du Huo Ji Sheng Wan (独活寄生丸) – Treats arthritis and joint pain.

  9. Wu Zi Yan Zong Wan (五子衍宗丸) – Male fertility tonic.

  10. Er Chen Wan (二陈丸) – Dissolves phlegm, used for chronic cough.

5. Topical & External Applications (外用方, Wai Yong Fang)

Ground herbs applied externally for healing.

  1. Ginger Compress (姜敷) – Crushed ginger applied for pain relief.

  2. Moxa Powder (艾粉) – Dried mugwort ground for moxibustion therapy.

  3. Coptis Root Paste (黄连糊) – For mouth ulcers and infections.

  4. Bai Bu Powder (百部粉) – Treats lice and scabies.

  5. She Chuang Zi Paste (蛇床子膏) – Treats fungal infections.

  6. Crushed Honeysuckle (金银花糊) – Applied for heat rashes.

  7. Cinnamon & Clove Poultice (肉桂丁香敷贴) – Warms and relieves muscle aches.

  8. Salt & Sichuan Pepper Foot Soak (盐花椒泡脚) – Warms the body and relieves pain.

  9. Crushed Lotus Seed Paste (莲子膏) – Applied for skin hydration.

  10. Realgar & Sulfur Powder (雄黄硫磺粉) – Used in TCM insect repellents.
